## Recommended report paragraph

> The project follows a single evidence chain rather than a set of independent charts. We first use recognition lag to show that Test of Time recognition is retrospective, then examine the venues, fields, and topics where such papers are most visible. Citation depth and impact breadth are used as complementary public-metadata signals, while Explorer and Benchmark Lab connect aggregate patterns back to individual papers. Finally, institution and country views describe the visible research ecosystem. Across all modules, metrics are treated as dataset-based observations and proxy indicators, not as official award causes.

## Presentation route

1. Opening summary: define Test of Time and the 250-paper sample.
2. Time: explain recognition lag and one short / typical / long case.
3. Venue and Field: show visible communities, with a caveat that this is not a ranking.
4. Topic Evolution: describe persistent foundations and later-visible application ecosystems.
5. Citation and Impact: separate depth, trajectory, and breadth.
6. Explorer and Benchmark: click one representative paper and show traceability.
7. Network: close with research-ecosystem concentration and metadata limits.
